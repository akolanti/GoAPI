THE DARK KNIGHT OF GOTHAM
A Story of Thomas

The city of Gotham never truly slept. Rain hammered the cobblestones as a lone
figure crouched atop the Gotham National Bank, his black cape rippling in the
wind. That figure was Thomas — the Batman.

By day, Thomas was an ordinary man with an extraordinary mind. He worked quietly,
drank his morning coffee, and exchanged polite nods with neighbors who had no
idea what he did when the sun went down.

But tonight, the Bat-Signal had cut through the storm clouds like a blade.

Commissioner Gordon's voice crackled through the comm: "Thomas, the Riddler's
men are moving a weapon through the East Docks. We have maybe twenty minutes."

"Fourteen," Thomas corrected, already gliding off the rooftop.

He moved like a shadow given purpose — swift, silent, deliberate. The grapple gun
hissed. The cape spread wide. Gotham blurred beneath him.

At the docks, seven armed men surrounded a steel crate. Thomas landed without
a sound behind the first guard. One breath. One motion. The man crumpled quietly.

Then it was a storm of a different kind.

Batarangs sang through the air. Boots struck with surgical precision. Crates
toppled. Men shouted, scrambled, and fell — one by one — before they ever saw
his face clearly. Only the cape. Only the symbol.

Within ninety seconds, it was over.

Thomas stood alone among the unconscious, breathing slowly, the rain washing
over the bat emblem on his chest. He opened the crate — inside, the weapon,
disarmed and harmless.

He pressed his comm. "Gordon. East Docks. Send a truck."

A pause. Then: "Every time, Batman. Every single time. How do you do it?"

Thomas allowed himself the smallest of smiles beneath the cowl.

"Preparation," he said. "And a very good cape."

He fired his grapple into the dark sky and was gone — swallowed by the rain
and the night, a guardian Gotham didn't always deserve, but one man named
Thomas had chosen to be anyway.
